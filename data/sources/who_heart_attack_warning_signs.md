Title: Heart Attack Warning Signs
Organization: World Health Organization
Source URL: https://www.who.int/westernpacific/newsroom/fact-sheets/detail/cardiovascular-diseases-%28cvds%29
Summary: Extracted and paraphrased WHO guidance on recognizing heart attack warning signs and the need for urgent care.
A possible heart attack may cause pain or discomfort in the centre of the chest and can spread to the arms, left shoulder, elbows, jaw, or back.

A person may also have shortness of breath, nausea or vomiting, light-headedness, a cold sweat, or sudden paleness.

Treat these warning signs as an emergency and activate emergency medical help without delay.

Keep the person at rest, monitor breathing and responsiveness, and be prepared to provide further first aid while urgent help is on the way.
