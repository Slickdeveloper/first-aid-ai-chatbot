Title: Muscle, Bone and Joint Injury
Organization: American Red Cross
Source URL: https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/resources/learn-first-aid/muscle-bone-joint-injury
Summary: Paraphrased guidance for suspected fractures, sprains, and dislocations based on the American Red Cross muscle, bone, and joint injury resource.

Treat all serious muscle, bone, and joint injuries as possible fractures.

Have the person rest and limit movement of the injured area. Do not try to straighten it.

If the injury involves the head, neck, or back, tell the person not to move unless safety, CPR, or bleeding control makes movement necessary.

For a closed injury, apply a cold pack wrapped in a thin, dry towel for no more than 20 minutes at a time.

For an open injury, control bleeding with direct pressure and seek urgent medical care for deformity, numbness, severe pain, or inability to move the area.
