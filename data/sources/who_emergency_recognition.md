Title: Community Emergency Recognition and First Response
Organization: World Health Organization
Source URL: https://www.who.int/teams/integrated-health-services/clinical-services-and-systems/emergency-and-critical-care/community-based-care
Summary: Extracted and paraphrased emergency first-response principles from WHO community-based emergency care guidance.
Community responders play an important role in early recognition of danger signs, first aid, and initiation of transfer to more definitive care.

Life-threatening conditions should be recognized quickly, simple first aid should begin early, and transport or referral should not be delayed.

People giving first response should monitor the person's breathing, responsiveness, and obvious danger signs while arranging access to higher-level care.

When the condition is severe or worsening, emergency services or formal health-system support should be activated immediately.
