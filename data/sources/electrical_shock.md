Title: Electrical Shock
Organization: Mayo Clinic
Source URL: https://www.mayoclinic.org/first-aid/first-aid-electrical-shock/basics/art-20056695
Summary: Paraphrased first-aid guidance for electrical shock based on Mayo Clinic guidance.

Turn off the source of electricity if possible, or use a dry nonconducting object such as cardboard, plastic, or wood to separate the source from the person.

Do not touch the person while they are still in contact with the current.

Call emergency services for severe burns, confusion, trouble breathing, irregular heartbeat, seizures, loss of consciousness, or suspected cardiac arrest.

If the person shows no signs of circulation such as breathing, coughing, or movement, begin CPR if you are trained, and cover burns with a sterile gauze dressing or clean cloth.
