Title: Heat Stroke
Organization: American Red Cross
Source URL: https://production.redcross.org/take-a-class/learn-fa-heat-stroke.html
Summary: Paraphrased heat-stroke emergency guidance based on the American Red Cross heat stroke resource.

Heat stroke is a life-threatening emergency. Call 9-1-1 immediately.

Begin rapid cooling right away by using cold-water immersion if it is safe and possible.

If immersion is not available, apply cold wet cloths or towels, place ice packs, and fan the person, or help them take a cold shower if appropriate.

Continue cooling for up to 20 minutes or until emergency responders arrive, while monitoring breathing and responsiveness.
