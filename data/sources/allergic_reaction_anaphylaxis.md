Title: Allergic Reaction and Anaphylaxis
Organization: American Red Cross
Source URL: https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/resources/learn-first-aid/allergic-reaction-anaphylaxis
Summary: Paraphrased severe allergic reaction guidance based on the American Red Cross anaphylaxis resource.

Anaphylaxis is a life-threatening allergic reaction that can cause swelling, trouble breathing, shock, or a change in responsiveness.

Call 9-1-1 for face, neck, tongue, or lip swelling, trouble breathing, or signs of shock.

Help the person use their prescribed epinephrine auto-injector, or administer it if you are trained and allowed to do so.

Keep checking breathing and responsiveness, give care for shock if needed, and be ready to help with a second dose if symptoms continue and EMS has not arrived.
