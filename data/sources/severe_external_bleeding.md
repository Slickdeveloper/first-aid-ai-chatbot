Title: Life-Threatening External Bleeding
Organization: American Red Cross
Source URL: https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/resources/learn-first-aid/bleeding-life-threatening-external
Summary: Paraphrased bleeding-control guidance based on the American Red Cross life-threatening external bleeding resource.

Life-threatening bleeding may involve continuous flow, spurting blood, or a large amount of blood loss.

Call 9-1-1 and apply steady direct pressure to the wound right away.

If the bleeding is on an arm or leg and is life-threatening, apply direct pressure and use a tourniquet if one is available and you are trained to use it.

If you are trained and a tourniquet cannot be used, consider wound packing for areas such as the scalp, neck, shoulder, groin, or back.

Keep the person from getting cold or overheated and watch for signs of shock until help arrives.
