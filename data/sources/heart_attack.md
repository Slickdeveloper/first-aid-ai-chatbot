Title: Heart Attack
Organization: American Red Cross
Source URL: https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/resources/learn-first-aid/heart-attack
Summary: Paraphrased heart-attack first-aid guidance based on the American Red Cross heart attack resource.

Call 9-1-1 immediately if you suspect a heart attack.

Signs and symptoms can include persistent chest pain or pressure, squeezing, tightness, aching or heaviness, pain spreading to the arms, neck, jaw, back or stomach, trouble breathing, pale or bluish skin, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, extreme fatigue, or sweating.

Have the person stop activity and rest in a comfortable position.

If they are awake, can understand simple commands, can chew or swallow, and are allowed to have aspirin, assist them in taking aspirin according to the Red Cross guidance.

Loosen tight clothing, stay with them, watch for changes in breathing or responsiveness, and be ready to give CPR and use an AED if they become unresponsive and are not breathing.
