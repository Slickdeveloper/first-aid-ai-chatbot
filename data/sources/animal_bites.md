Title: Animal Bites
Organization: American Red Cross
Source URL: https://www.redcross.org/take-a-class/resources/learn-first-aid/animal-bites
Summary: Paraphrased animal-bite first-aid guidance based on the American Red Cross animal bites resource.

If the bite causes life-threatening bleeding, give care for life-threatening external bleeding right away.

If the skin is broken, clean the wound with soap and water and seek medical care because antibiotics, rabies vaccine, or a tetanus shot may be needed.

For minor wounds, use direct pressure for minor bleeding, clean and flush the wound with soap and water, and cover it appropriately.

Call 9-1-1 for severe bleeding or if the bite is from a stray or wild animal that could have rabies.
